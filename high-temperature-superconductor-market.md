<p><p>The high-temperature superconductor (HTS) market is primarily divided into two categories: 1G HTS and 2G HTS. </p><p>1G HTS refers to first-generation superconductors, typically consisting of bulk materials or wires, known for their high critical current density but limited flexibility and performance in applications. </p><p>2G HTS involves second-generation superconductors, characterized by their thin films and enhanced properties, offering better flexibility, higher critical temperatures, and greater application potential in power transmission, magnetic resonance imaging, and transport systems.</p></p>
