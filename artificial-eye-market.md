<p><p>The artificial eye market comprises two primary types: non-integrated implants and integrated implants. Non-integrated implants are standalone devices that mimic the appearance of a natural eye but do not restore vision, primarily focusing on cosmetic enhancement. In contrast, integrated implants are advanced systems that combine surgical techniques and technology to restore partial vision or functionality. These devices often utilize electronic components to enhance visual signals, making them more suitable for patients with specific visual impairments.</p></p>
