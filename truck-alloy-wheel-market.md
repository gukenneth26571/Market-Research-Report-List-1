<p><p>The truck alloy wheel market encompasses three main types: casting, forging, and others. </p><p>Casting involves pouring molten metal into molds to create complex shapes, offering cost-effectiveness and versatility for mass production. Forging, on the other hand, utilizes high-pressure to shape metal, resulting in stronger and more durable wheels due to the enhanced grain structure. The "other" category includes alternative manufacturing methods like rolling or machining, catering to specific performance needs or aesthetic preferences. Each method serves distinct applications in the trucking industry.</p></p>
