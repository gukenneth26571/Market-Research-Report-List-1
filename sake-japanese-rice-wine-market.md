<p><p>The Japanese sake market features various types, each distinguished by brewing methods and rice polishing rates. Ordinary sake is a basic form with less emphasis on quality. Junmai is pure rice sake, offering robust flavors. Honjozo includes a small amount of distilled alcohol, enhancing aroma and flavor. Junmai Ginjo is crafted from highly polished rice, delivering a refined taste. Ginjo, similarly polished, emphasizes fruity and floral notes, appealing to connoisseurs. Each type caters to diverse consumer preferences and occasions.</p></p>
