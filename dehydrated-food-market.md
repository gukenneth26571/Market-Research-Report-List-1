<p><p>The dehydrated food market comprises several drying methods, each with unique characteristics. Spray dried food involves atomizing food solutions into hot air, preserving nutrients and flavors. Freeze dried food retains texture and taste by sublimating frozen water. Vacuum dried food eliminates moisture at reduced pressure, enhancing quality. Sun dried foods utilize natural sunlight, though they may be less controlled. Hot air dried foods circulate warm air to remove moisture efficiently. Other methods include osmotic drying and microwave methods, catering to diverse consumer needs.</p></p>
