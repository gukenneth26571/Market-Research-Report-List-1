<p><p>The duct damper market comprises two primary types: manual dampers and electric dampers. Manual dampers are operated by hand, allowing users to control airflow within HVAC systems easily. They are typically more affordable and require no electrical power. In contrast, electric dampers utilize motors for automated control, offering enhanced precision and efficiency in airflow regulation. Electric dampers are often integrated into smart systems, providing better energy management, while manual dampers are favored for straightforward applications and lower upfront costs.</p></p>
