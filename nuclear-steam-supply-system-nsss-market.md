<p><p>The Nuclear Steam Supply System (NSSS) refers to the components of a nuclear power plant that generate steam for driving turbines, primarily featuring systems like pressurized water reactors (PWR), boiling water reactors (BWR), and pressurized heavy water reactors (PHWR). PWRs use pressurized water to transfer heat, while BWRs boil water directly in the reactor core. PHWRs utilize heavy water as a coolant and moderator, enabling efficient fuel usage. Each type serves distinct markets, driven by varying operational efficiencies and regulatory environments.</p></p>
