<p><p>Calcium Chloride (CaCl2) is a versatile compound used in various industries, with two main market types: Calcium Chloride Dihydrate and Calcium Chloride Anhydrous. Calcium Chloride Dihydrate contains two water molecules and is commonly utilized for de-icing, dust control, and as a drying agent. In contrast, Calcium Chloride Anhydrous lacks water and is primarily used in applications requiring a concentrated form, such as in chemical synthesis and as a desiccant. Both forms serve critical roles across agricultural, construction, and industrial sectors.</p></p>
