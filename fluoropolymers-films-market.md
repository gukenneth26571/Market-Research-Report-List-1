<p><p>Fluoropolymer films are categorized into several types based on their chemical structure and properties. Polytetrafluoroethylene (PTFE) is known for its non-stick and high-temperature resistance. Fluorinated Ethylene Propylene (FEP) offers excellent chemical resistance and clarity. Perfluoroalkoxy Alkane (PFA) combines the properties of PTFE and FEP, providing flexibility and thermal stability. Polyvinyl Fluoride (PVF) is valued for its durability and toughness, while Polyvinylidene Fluoride (PVDF) is recognized for its mechanical strength and chemical resistance. Ethylene Tetrafluoroethylene (ETFE) exhibits high strength and transparency. Other variants include specialty formulations suited for niche applications.</p></p>
