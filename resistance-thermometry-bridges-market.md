<p><p>Resistance thermometry bridges are measurement devices used for precise temperature determinations by comparing resistances. The market is divided into two primary types: alternating current (AC) and direct current (DC). AC bridges utilize varying current to minimize measurement errors and factors like thermal emf, making them ideal for high precision applications. In contrast, DC bridges provide stable readings and are straightforward in design, suitable for general use. Both types serve crucial roles across industries requiring accurate thermal measurements.</p></p>
