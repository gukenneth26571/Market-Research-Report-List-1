<p><p>The Bare Copper Conductor market is categorized into Soft-Drawn, Medium-Hard-Drawn, and Hard-Drawn types based on tensile strength and flexibility. Soft-Drawn copper is highly malleable, ideal for applications requiring flexibility, such as wiring and cables. Medium-Hard-Drawn offers a balance between strength and flexibility, suitable for overhead lines and grounding. Hard-Drawn copper has high tensile strength, used in rigid applications like busbars and transmission lines. Each type caters to specific industrial needs, balancing conductivity, durability, and mechanical properties.</p></p>
