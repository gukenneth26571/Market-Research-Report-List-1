<p><p>The residential heat meters market is primarily divided into two types: smart heat meters and mechanical heat meters. Smart heat meters utilize digital technology to provide real-time data on heating consumption, enabling advanced features like remote monitoring and automated billing. In contrast, mechanical heat meters operate using traditional measuring techniques, typically relying on mechanical components to gauge heat usage. While smart meters offer enhanced accuracy and user engagement, mechanical meters are often more cost-effective, appealing to budget-conscious consumers.</p></p>
