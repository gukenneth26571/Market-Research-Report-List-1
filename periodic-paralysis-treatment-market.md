<p><p>The periodic paralysis treatment market encompasses various therapeutic categories, primarily including beta-blockers, carbonic anhydrase inhibitors, and other treatments. Beta-blockers are commonly used to manage symptoms by stabilizing cardiac function and reducing muscle excitability. Carbonic anhydrase inhibitors help regulate electrolytes, particularly potassium levels, thereby preventing episodes. Other treatments may include potassium supplements, dietary changes, or lifestyle modifications. Together, these approaches aim to improve patient outcomes, reduce attack frequency, and enhance quality of life for individuals with periodic paralysis.</p></p>
