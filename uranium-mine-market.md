<p><ul><li>In Situ Leach Mining (ISL)</li><li>Open-pit Mining</li><li>Underground Mining</li><li>By-product</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The uranium mine market features several key types of mining methods. In Situ Leach Mining (ISL) involves extracting uranium by injecting a leaching solution into the groundwater, which dissolves the minerals for recovery. Open-pit mining entails removing surface layers to access uranium deposits, suitable for shallow reserves. Underground mining involves tunneling to reach deeper ore deposits, usually more labor-intensive. Lastly, the by-product market refers to uranium extracted as a secondary product from mining operations primarily targeting other minerals, contributing to overall supply.</p></p>
